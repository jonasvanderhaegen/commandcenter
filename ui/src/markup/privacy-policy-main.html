Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Osmo ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our website and services. By using the Osmo website, you agree to the terms outlined in this Privacy Policy. If you disagree with any part, you may not access our services.

We take your privacy seriously. This policy breaks down what data we collect, how we use it, and what your options are to stay in control.

Information We Collect

  • Non-Personal Data: We collect non-personal information, such as your browser type, language preferences, and the date/time of your visit. This helps us improve your experience and ensure everything works as it should.
  • Personal Data: When you interact with Osmo (e.g., signing up, making a purchase, or contacting us), we may collect:
    • Name and email address.
    • Payment information (processed securely by Stripe).
    • Other optional data you share with us (e.g., survey responses or preferences).

We don't share your personally identifying information publicly or with third parties, except when required by law.

How We Use Your Information

  • To provide and improve our services.
  • To process payments and deliver products or subscriptions.
  • To send you updates about new resources or important changes (only the ones you want).

Protection of Information

Osmo discloses potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information only to those of its employees, contractors, and affiliated organizations that:

  1. Have agreed to confidentiality obligations that prevent disclosure to others.
  2. Need to know that information to process it on Osmo's behalf or provide services available through the Osmo platform, and

Some of these employees, contractors, and affiliated organizations may be located outside your home country. By using Osmo's website, you consent to the transfer of your information to them.

‍

Osmo does not rent or sell potentially personally-identifying or personally-identifying information to anyone. Outside of the parties mentioned above, Osmo discloses such information only in the following cases:

  • Legal Requirements: When required to comply with legal obligations or governmental requests.
  • Good Faith Belief: When Osmo believes disclosure is reasonably necessary to protect its rights, property, or the safety of its users, third parties, or the public.

We strive to handle your data responsibly and in line with best practices to maintain your trust and privacy.

We don't train AI with your data

We don't use your data to train AI models. Does that make us the only one in 2025? We're not sure, but your data stays private.
